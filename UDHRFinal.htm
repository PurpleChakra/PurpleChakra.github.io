Welcoming Cities

Almost 2600 asylum seekers are currently being held in offshore detention centres or regional processing centres, with just under half of them being confined on Nauru and Manus Island.1 These ‘holding’ facilities retain those waiting for visa approval from the government; however, 44.3% of people in these facilities have been languishing for over 2 years, with the overall average wait time currently sitting at 493 days, according to Government statistics.2

Asylum seekers are not only being held for long periods of time, they are living in conditions that are inhumane. In the words of a guard who worked at Manus, the asylum seekers’ living environment is ‘filthy’, and he was ‘disgusted’ by what he saw.3 The Australian Government’s treatment of these vulnerable people breaches over half of articles in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), the most severe of which are discussed below.4

Article 2: Freedom from Discrimination

Article 2 states that no person may be discriminated against or denied any rights for any reason, including race and country of origin. By their horrendous treatment of those seeking asylum in Australia, our government is perpetrating racism and discrimination, and degrading Australia’s reputation as a free, fair and multicultural country.

Article 5: Freedom from Torture and Degrading Treatment; Article 25: Right to an Adequate Living Standard

The conditions on Manus and Nauru have been shown time and again5,6,7 to be squalid, cruel and inhumane, directly opposing Article 5’s assertion that ‘No one shall be subjected to… inhuman or degrading treatment’. By the Government’s allowance of the conditions inflicted on asylum seekers to continue, they are disregarding one of the most basic rights: the right to an adequate living standard.

Article 14: Right to Asylum from Persecution in other Countries

Due to various factors like war, poverty, political oppression and terror, people are displaced from their countries of origin and seek asylum in other countries. Australia has a policy to ensure that people who do not have the right to seek asylum are denied entery, but the long wait times and the conditions forced upon those waiting make this process a real problem, compounding the issues discussed in relation to the previous articles.

It is Welcome to Australia’s belief that the vetting process can be changed to allow for all those seeking asylum can be held in conditions more appropriate to their status as a fellow human being.

Article 30: Freedom from State or Personal Interference with the Above Rights

Encompassing the first 27 Articles in the UDHR are the final three, the last of which being Article 30. Article 30 ensures that no power may interfere with anybody’s receipt of any of the rights in the Declaration. This is clearly breached by the Australian Government, as discussed in relation with the previous articles.

How can I help?

Due to the government’s role in the upkeep and continued existence of these detention centres, applying political pressure on Members of Parliament is an option for the removal or discontinuation of places like Manus and Nauru.

By calling or writing to your local MP, you can help to end the outrageous treatment of asylum seeker and make this world a better place. You can also sign our petition to the Australian Government.
